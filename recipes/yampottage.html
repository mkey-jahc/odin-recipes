Jollof Rice

Description

If you're a novice Nigerian trying to make it in this Naija life, you need to have a staple Nigerian dish to present.

You want your guests to eat sleep and repeat.

Look no further. YAM POTTAGE is here :)

It is classic. It is hearty. It is simple.

Feast on...

credit to https://www.thepretendchef.com/yam-porridge/

Ingredients

  • Meat, dry fish, stockfish, smoked fish, cow leg, bone marrow or tripe etc. (optional)
  • 730g yam
  • 3 cups water + stock
  • 3/4 cup chopped onions, (110g)
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • salt
  • 1 scotch bonnet (fresh pepper)
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 4g ogili okpei (even I don't know what this is)
  • 6 tablespoons palm oil
  1. Cut and peel yam, cut into cubes and rinse in a bowl of water.
  2. Place yam in a pot, add water and stock, enough to just cover the yam.
  3. Add meat or dry fish, onions, seasoning cubes, salt and pepper. (Not too much salt)
  4. Place over medium-high heat. Add crayfish, ground
  5. Add crayfish, ground ogili okpei and palm oil. Stir and cover.
  6. Cook till yam is very soft.
  7. The slurry will be light and watery at this stage.
  8. Use a wooden spoon, if one is available, and stir your yam porridge continuously.
  9. Take out 1 or 2 pieces of yam, mash them and stir it into the pot.
  10. Reduce the heat and cook for a short while, taste and take off the heat.
